About me

Hey there. I'm Octavio, pleased to meet you!

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Anthropometrics and Historical Epidemiology Group at the Institute of Evolutionary Medicine, University of Zurich, Switzerland. Previously I was at the UTMB Sealy Center on Aging of the University of Texas Medical Branch, analyzing trends, causes, and inequalities in health and aging from an international perspective.

I earned my Ph.D. in Demography at the Centre d'Estudis Demogràfics (CED), Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona — cum laude with International Doctorate mention — sponsored by a FI-AGAUR doctoral grant. I also hold a Master's in Demography (UAB/Max Planck), a Master's in Social Demography (Universidad Nacional de Luján), a Bachelor's in Sociology (Universidad de Buenos Aires), and completed the European Doctoral School of Demography (EDSD).

My main interests are health and mortality, demographic and statistical methods applied to social science, social inequalities in aging, and the design of equitable pension and public health systems. I know I said health a lot in that sentence.

I have worked as a Population Data Analyst at ANSES (Social Security and Pension Bureau of Argentina) and have been appointed as consultant by the World Bank and the United Nations Population Fund, among other consulting work.

Outside research: sports (a lot), music (also a lot), films, coffee (probably more need than like), mate, random pop-culture references, and terrible jokes that people laugh at anyway. So nice of them.

Research

What I work on

My work sits at the intersection of demographic methods, epidemiology, and public health, with a focus on population-level patterns of mortality and healthy aging across time, space, and social groups.

📊

Mortality & longevity trends

Cause-specific mortality, life expectancy decomposition, birth cohort patterns across high-income countries.

🧠

Healthy aging & disability

Dementia-free life expectancy, functional limitations, ADL/IADL trajectories and multistate modeling.

📐

Demographic methods

Multistate life tables, Age-Period-Cohort modeling, Sullivan method, Lexis surface visualization.

⚖️

Health inequalities

Educational, racial/ethnic, and socioeconomic disparities in mortality and aging — US, Europe, Latin America.

🌎

Latin American demography

Mortality trends in Argentina and Mexico, cause-of-death analysis, historical epidemiology.

🏥

Historical epidemiology

Swiss historical mortality, prenatal pandemic exposure effects, psychiatric and neurological outcomes.
